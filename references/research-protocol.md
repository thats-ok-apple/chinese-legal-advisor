# 现行法检索与引用规范

## Source order

1. Start with the National Laws and Regulations Database (`flk.npc.gov.cn`) for laws, administrative regulations, and local regulations.
2. Verify judicial interpretations and court procedure on Supreme People's Court or other court websites; verify prosecutorial procedure on Supreme People's Procuratorate sources.
3. Verify ministry-administered rules and national complaint channels on the responsible State Council department's website.
4. Verify local rules, enforcement division, service hotlines, and online complaint routes on the provincial or municipal people's government, local legislature, court, or responsible authority's official site.
5. Use a reliable secondary source only to locate an official source. Label it as secondary and do not rely on it alone for a material conclusion.

## Verification rules

- Check title, issuing body, article number, effective date, amendment/repeal status, and territorial scope before citing a provision.
- Search separately for the sanction provision. A rule that prohibits conduct may not authorize a fine against the actor.
- Check whether a local rule changes the definition of a prohibited place, responsible entity, competent authority, reporting number, or penalty.
- Cite a direct page or official database record beside every material legal conclusion. State the date checked when currency matters.
- Do not cite unverifiable case numbers, articles, penalties, or agencies. Say that no sufficient official source was found and describe the next authoritative source to check.

## Practical evidence and safety

- Preserve original documents and unedited media. Record when and where each item was obtained and avoid publishing others' personal information.
- Prefer a written request or complaint that states facts, requested action, evidence, and contact details. Keep a copy and receipt number.
- Treat immediate danger, violence, fire risk, medical urgency, or a live criminal incident as an emergency matter; direct the user to the appropriate emergency service rather than a routine complaint path.
